## NEM 3.0 (Net Billing Tariff) Overview
- NEM 3.0 took effect April 15, 2023 for new solar installations interconnecting with California's three major IOUs (PG&E, SCE, SDG&E).
- LADWP customers are NOT subject to NEM 3.0 — LADWP has its own net metering program with near-retail export rates (~$0.22–$0.34/kWh).

## Key Changes from NEM 2.0
- Export compensation rates reduced by approximately 75% compared to NEM 2.0.
- SCE export credits now average ~$0.05–$0.08/kWh under NEM 3.0.
- Credits are based on the "Avoided Cost Calculator" — value of exported energy varies by time of day and month.
- Exports during evening peak hours (4–9 PM) are worth more than midday exports.
- ACC Plus adders provide additional compensation but decrease by 20% annually through 2028.

## NEM 2.0 Grandfathering — CRITICAL DEADLINE
- NEM 2.0 customers are grandfathered for 20 years from their interconnection date.
- DEADLINE: April 15, 2026 is the FINAL date to achieve Permission to Operate (PTO) and still qualify for NEM 2.0 rates.
- Systems that applied for interconnection by April 14, 2023 have until April 15, 2026 to connect and qualify for NEM 2.0.
- After this date, all new systems will be on NEM 3.0.

## Impact on Solar Design
- Battery storage is now CRITICAL for maximizing savings under NEM 3.0.
- Systems should be designed to maximize self-consumption rather than grid export.
- Time-of-use optimization: store midday solar, discharge during peak evening hours.
- Typical payback period under NEM 3.0: 6–9 years (with battery), compared to 4–6 years under NEM 2.0.

## Legal Status (2026)
- NEM 3.0 is under active legal review — the California Supreme Court unanimously ordered the Court of Appeal to reconsider its legality in August 2025.
- A ruling is expected by mid-2026.
- The CPUC's three-year review of the Net Billing Tariff starts after April 2026, which could modify export rates or add fixed charges.

## Business Customers
- Starting March 2026, new solar business customers will be billed on the Solar Billing Plan (NEM 3.0 equivalent for commercial).
