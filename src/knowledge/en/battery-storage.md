## Why Battery Storage?
- Store excess solar energy for use during evening peak hours (saves money under NEM 3.0)
- Backup power during grid outages and PSPS (Public Safety Power Shutoff) events
- Reduce dependence on the grid
- Maximize self-consumption of solar energy
- Critical for optimal savings under California's NEM 3.0 policy

## Tesla Powerwall 3 — 2026 Pricing & Specs
- Capacity: 13.5 kWh usable per unit
- Power output: 11.5 kW continuous
- Integrated solar inverter
- Whole-home backup capable
- 10-year warranty
- Price: ~$11,500 – $16,500 fully installed (unit ~$8,000–$11,000 + installation labor ~$2,000–$6,000 + accessories ~$800–$1,500)
- Prices tend to be higher in coastal California markets due to labor and permit costs
- Nason Solar is a Tesla Certified Installer

## How Many Batteries Do I Need?
- 1 battery: Partial home backup, peak-shaving for savings
- 2 batteries: Whole-home backup for most homes
- 3+ batteries: Large homes or full off-grid capability
- Our team will design the right system based on your usage and backup needs

## SGIP Rebates — 2026 Update
- SGIP's ratepayer-funded budgets CLOSED December 31, 2025.
- The only remaining pathway is the RSSE AB 209 program ($280M state budget) for low-income households only.
- RSSE is currently fully reserved; new applicants go on a waitlist.
- For most non-low-income homeowners, SGIP battery rebates are no longer available.
- The federal 30% ITC for batteries has also expired as of January 1, 2026.
