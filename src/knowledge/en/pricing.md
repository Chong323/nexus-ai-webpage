## Residential Solar Pricing (Before Incentives) — 2026
- Typical cost: $2.50 – $3.50 per watt installed
- Average home system size: 8 – 12 kW
- Estimated total: $20,000 – $42,000 before any incentives
- IMPORTANT: The 30% federal tax credit (ITC) has expired as of January 1, 2026. These prices reflect the full out-of-pocket cost for homeowner-owned systems.
- Third-party owned systems (leases/PPAs) may still benefit from business tax credits through 2027, potentially resulting in lower rates for the homeowner.
- Exact pricing depends on: roof type, system size, equipment selection, electrical upgrades needed

## Battery Storage Pricing — 2026
- **Tesla Powerwall 3:** ~$11,500 – $16,500 fully installed per unit
  - Unit cost: ~$8,000 – $11,000 before installation
  - Installation labor: ~$2,000 – $6,000
  - Accessories: ~$800 – $1,500
  - Note: Prices tend to be higher in coastal California markets
- **Enphase IQ Battery 5P:** ~$7,500 – $8,500 per unit installed; a two-battery system runs ~$15,000 – $17,000 installed
  - The IQ Battery 5P is Enphase's current-generation product, replacing the older IQ Battery 10
- Most homes need 1–2 battery units for meaningful backup

## EV Charger Pricing
- Level 2 EV Charger (residential): ~$1,500 – $3,000 installed
- Includes charger unit, electrical work, and permitting

## Commercial Solar
- Commercial pricing varies significantly based on system size and complexity.
- Typical range: $1.80 – $2.80 per watt for larger systems (50kW+).
- Commercial ITC (30%) still available for projects beginning construction by July 4, 2026.
- Contact our commercial team for a custom proposal.

## What Affects Your Price
- Roof condition and material (tile, shingle, flat)
- Panel and inverter brand selection
- Electrical panel upgrade requirements ($1,500 – $3,500 if needed)
- Permit and HOA requirements
- Battery storage inclusion
- System size (larger systems have lower per-watt cost)
