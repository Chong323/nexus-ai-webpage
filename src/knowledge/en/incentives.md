## Federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC)
- IMPORTANT: The federal ITC (Investment Tax Credit) has EXPIRED and is NO LONGER available for new residential solar installations.
- If customers ask about the "30% tax credit," clearly state that this program has ended.
- Customers who installed solar before the expiration may still claim credits for their tax year.

## California SGIP (Self-Generation Incentive Program)
- SGIP provides rebates for battery storage systems (not solar panels alone).
- Rebate amounts vary by utility territory and budget availability.
- Higher rebates available for customers in high fire-threat districts or on medical baseline.
- Check with your utility (SCE, LADWP, PG&E) for current SGIP budget status.

## California Net Energy Metering (see nem3.md for details)
- NEM 3.0 is now in effect — reduced export rates make battery storage more important than ever.

## Utility-Specific Programs
- **SCE (Southern California Edison):** Various time-of-use rate plans optimized for solar.
- **LADWP:** Separate net metering program, generally more favorable export rates than IOUs.
- **PG&E:** NEM 3.0 applies, battery strongly recommended.

## Property Tax Exemption
- California's Solar Property Tax Exclusion: solar installations do NOT increase your property tax assessment.
- This exclusion applies through 2025 (check for extensions).
