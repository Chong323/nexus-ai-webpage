## Federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) — Updated 2026
- The residential ITC (Section 25D) EXPIRED on December 31, 2025. Homeowner-owned solar systems installed in 2026 or later are NO LONGER eligible for the 30% federal tax credit.
- The "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" (signed July 2025) terminated key aspects of the ITC for solar and wind projects.
- HOWEVER, third-party owned systems (leases and PPAs) may still qualify for tax credits through the end of 2027 via the Section 48E business credit. The savings may be passed through to homeowners as lower lease/PPA rates.
- Commercial/utility-scale solar: The 30% ITC remains available for projects that begin physical construction by July 4, 2026, or are placed in service by December 31, 2027. The "five percent safe harbor" has been removed for projects over 1.5 MW — physical work must be demonstrated.
- Customers who installed solar BEFORE January 1, 2026 may still claim credits on their tax returns for the year of installation.

## California SGIP (Self-Generation Incentive Program) — Updated 2026
- As of December 31, 2025, SGIP's ratepayer-funded budgets (General Market, Equity, Equity Resiliency) are CLOSED to new applications.
- The only active pathway in 2026 is the **RSSE AB 209 program** — a $280 million state-funded budget exclusively for low-income households.
- RSSE can cover up to ~$21,700 toward solar and ~$11,000 toward battery storage for qualifying households, potentially covering 100% of installation costs.
- The RSSE budget is fully reserved as of early 2026; new applicants are placed on a waitlist and funded as existing reservations cancel.
- For most non-low-income homeowners, SGIP rebates are no longer available.

## California Net Energy Metering (see nem3.md for details)
- NEM 3.0 is in effect — reduced export rates make battery storage critical.
- IMPORTANT DEADLINE: NEM 2.0 grandfathering ends April 15, 2026. Systems that do not have Permission to Operate (PTO) by this date will fall under NEM 3.0 rates.

## Utility-Specific Programs
- **SCE (Southern California Edison):** NEM 3.0 applies. Export credits average ~$0.05–$0.08/kWh (roughly 75% less than NEM 2.0). Various TOU rate plans available.
- **LADWP (Los Angeles):** Municipal utility, NOT subject to NEM 3.0. Still offers near-retail rate net metering (~$0.22–$0.34/kWh export credits). No current solar rebate program (last program ran out of funding in 2018).
- **PG&E:** NEM 3.0 applies, battery strongly recommended.

## Property Tax Exemption
- California's Solar Property Tax Exclusion: solar installations do NOT increase your property tax assessment.
- Current sunset date: January 1, 2027. Systems installed before this date are excluded from reassessment.
- No extension has been enacted as of early 2026. If you are considering solar, installing before 2027 ensures you benefit from this exclusion.
